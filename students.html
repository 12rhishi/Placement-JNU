For Students

Rules, guidelines, and resources for JNU students participating in campus placements.

Rules and Guidelines for Students

  • Students should regularly visit the JNU Placement Cell website for jobs, internships, and training updates.
  • Interested students must register with the Placement Cell by filling the Student Registration Form (see below).
  • All registered students will receive emails about jobs, internships, and events.
  • Only registered students of JNU are allowed to participate in Campus Placement.
  • All information related to Campus Recruitment, Jobs, and Internships will be available on the JNU Placement Cell webpage.
  • The Placement Cell will not be responsible for absenteeism in any test, interview, or selection process required by the company.

Frequently Asked Questions

How will I come to know about job vacancies?

The JNU Placement Cell posts job announcements on its website. Registered students also receive email notifications about jobs, internships, and events.

How may I avail the JNU Placement Cell support?

To access support and opportunities, you must register with the Placement Cell by filling the Student Registration Form available on this page.

How many organisations can I apply for?

You can apply to as many companies as you like until you are placed.

Are there any school-specific organisations invited?

Yes, both school-specific and multi-disciplinary organizations are invited to conduct placement drives.

How do organisations shortlist programs they wish to interact with?

Candidates are chosen from a pool of applicants based on their CVs.

Can I apply for M.Tech placement drives if I am not pursuing M.Tech but have done B.Tech in CSE/ECE?

It depends on the organization. If the role is strictly for M.Tech students, you are not allowed to apply. If the organization is also looking for B.Tech graduates, then you may apply.

What kind of information do students expect in Pre-Placement Talks?
  • Profile and reputation of the corporation
  • Career roles and responsibilities offered in different types of profiles
  • Locations where the student is likely to be posted
  • Compensation packages

The pre-placement talk is a platform for interaction between the organisation and the students, followed by a Q&A session for better understanding of the organisation and potential roles.
